## Overview

OctoAcme follows a structured, iterative project management lifecycle designed to deliver customer value reliably and repeatably. Every project moves through five key phases: **Initiation**, **Planning**, **Execution & Tracking**, **Release & Deployment**, and **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement**. Each phase has defined deliverables, decision gates, and owners, ensuring that work is always grounded in a clear problem statement, measurable success criteria, and stakeholder alignment before effort is committed.

The team is built around clearly defined roles and personas: the **Project Manager (PM)** coordinates schedules, risks, and cross-team communications; the **Product Manager (PdM)** owns the roadmap and backlog prioritization; **Developers** design, implement, and test features; **QA/Testing** validates acceptance criteria; and **Stakeholders** provide inputs and approvals. Each persona has explicit responsibilities, goals, and communication norms documented in the Roles & Personas guide, so team members and collaborators always know who to engage and when.

Communication is treated as a first-class practice at OctoAcme. A regular cadence—weekly PM/PdM syncs, twice-weekly delivery standups, and monthly stakeholder updates—keeps everyone aligned throughout the lifecycle. Risk identification and escalation are woven into every phase via a living Risk Register, and the Risks & Communication guide outlines how to surface, assess, and respond to emerging issues before they impact delivery.

Quality assurance and continuous improvement close the loop. Acceptance criteria and a Definition of Done are agreed upon during planning and validated by QA before every release. After each release, the team holds a structured retrospective to capture learnings, celebrate wins, and commit to actionable improvements. This culture of reflection ensures that OctoAcme's processes evolve alongside the team and the product.
